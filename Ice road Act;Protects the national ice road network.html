Etiquette

Penalties

While breaking any etiquette in this article has no penalty on its own, a fine of up to 3G per offence may be issued to repeat offenders.

Boat clutter

If there is already a boat in a station, use that boat instead of placing a new one.

Boat return

If a station has a spot for boats to go after use, all boats must be pushed thereafter disembarking if possible.

Congestion

Boats may not be left on an ice road unattended.

Regulation

Stations

Security

A station must be accessible by all nation members. A station may be accessible by allies. A station must not be accessible by outsiders. There must be no way to bypass a stationâ€™s security. If a station is found to be insecure a fine of up to 20G may be issued, if 7 days later the station is still insecure another fine of up to 20G may be issued and the offending town may be disconnected from the network. If a station is breached a fine of up to 50G may be issued and the offending town may be disconnected from the network. If a station is not accessible by nation members a fine of up to 5G per day may be issued unless the station is closed. A fine of up to 25G may be issued for publishing the location of an ice road.

Closures

A station may be closed to all people for up to 7 days without penalty. A judge may increase the maximum time that a station can be closed on a case-by-case basis. If a station is closed for longer than the maximum time it can be closed for a fine of up to 5G per day may be issued.

Ice roads

Construction

Ice roads must be built in a 3X3 tunnel without any holes. On the bottom layer, there must be one block of ice in the middle every two meters, surrounded by slabs. The type of slab must be the same for the entire stretch of ice road. On the middle layer, there must be glass panes on either side. on the top layer, there must be torches on both sides where needed.

Modification

A fine of up to 5G for every 10 meter stretch of ice road affected may be issued for making an ice road no longer comply with subsection 2.2.1.
